Do not circulate it. Keep it safe, record details, and consult official channels for confirmation.

What to do if you suspect FAKE currency

  1. Do not circulate the note. Keep it separate to avoid legal risk.
  2. Note the source. Where/when you received it, amount, and context.
  3. Take clear photos. Front + back in good lighting (no glare).
  4. Verify again. Use AI verification + manual checks.
  5. Report safely. Visit your bank/authorized authority for guidance.

Image capture tips (for best AI accuracy)

  • Place the note on a flat surface under bright, even light.
  • Capture the full note with visible corners (no cropping).
  • Avoid shadows, flash glare, and tilted angles.
  • Use higher resolution; keep the camera steady.
  • Upload JPG/PNG only. Clean the lens for sharper details.

Common signs of counterfeit notes

Counterfeit notes often fail at least one security feature check. Use these as quick indicators.

Watermark

Looks printed, blurry, or not embedded when held against light.

Security Thread

Thread is missing, unreadable, or appears pasted on the surface.

Micro-lettering

Letters look thick/broken or are unreadable under magnification.

Color-shifting Ink

No visible color change when you tilt the note.

Print Quality

Smudged ink, uneven lines, or unusual texture/feel.

Alignment

Design elements look misaligned or inconsistent between sides.

Myth vs Fact

Myth

“One feature is enough to confirm.”

Checking multiple security features is recommended.

Fact

Check several features

Use “feel/look/tilt” style checks + compare with a known genuine note.

Myth

“Detector pens always work.”

Some methods are not reliable for many modern note materials.
